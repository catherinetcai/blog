TIL - Unicode Surrogate Pairs

Today, I had to work on a story dealing with emojis for work. Essentially, it had to deal with parsing emojis and flagging certain ones as banned. I wasn’t sure what format the emojis were going to come in as in order to test them against the code. I was expecting something along these lines: “U+1F4B0” or “\xF0\x9F\x92\xB0” (for the curious, the emoji is ðŸ’°). In any case, I got something that came back as u\D83DDCB0. Cue my confusion. After looking it up, I stumbled across . This article explains the design for the use of 16-bit Unicode values. The first value is the high surrogate and the second value is the low surrogate.  I’m not going to delve into the details, because I certainly didn’t need to… but it was just a good TIL to understand what the heck was going on when I was getting back weird strings instead of the Unicode I was expecting.
